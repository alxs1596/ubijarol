Biometric Passport

The biometric passport, also known as an electronic passport, is an identity document that, in addition to the use of security paper, contains a polycarbonate sheet with an electronic circuit embedded in it, and uses biometrics to authenticate travelers' citizenship.

Biometric Universal

The United Nations establishes the use of a chip to identify everyone in the world. The system collects facials, iris, and biometric fingerprint data, establishing itself in the only official documentation for refugees.

BIOCHIP

A biochip is a small-scale device, analogous to an integrated circuit, assembled or used to analyze organic molecules associated with living organisms. In theory a biochip is a bee built of large organic molecules, such as proteins, capable of performing the functions of a computer (data storage, processing).
